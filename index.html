🎮 Privilege Game

Welcome to the Privilege Game!

This is an interactive educational game designed to help players understand social dynamics, privilege, and inequality through gameplay. Navigate through different spaces, complete challenges, and learn about real-world perspectives.

How to Play

1. START: Begin your journey by rolling dice and moving around the board.

2. GUESS Spaces: Answer questions about privilege and social issues.

3. CARD Spaces: Draw a mystery card to learn surprising facts.

4. MOVE Spaces: Follow special instructions that may help or challenge your progress.

📚 Help & Guide

🎯 Space Types

  • START (Green): Beginning points where players enter the game
  • GUESS (Orange): Answer trivia questions to advance
  • CARD (Purple): Draw mystery cards with facts and scenarios
  • MOVE (Red): Special instructions - move forward, back, or swap positions

🔗 Connections System

Players form connections by landing on the same spaces. These connections create bonds that can be used strategically for mutual support or challenge cards. Track your connections to unlock special abilities!

🎴 Mystery Cards

Mystery cards contain real-world scenarios, facts about privilege, and challenges. Some cards give you advantages, while others present difficult choices that reflect actual social issues.

💡 Tips & Strategies

  • Pay attention to patterns - they reveal game mechanics
  • Build alliances with other players strategically
  • Answer GUESS questions honestly for better understanding
  • Learn from mystery cards - they teach real perspectives
  • Reflection is key - think about what each scenario means

❓ Q&A - Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I win the game?

A: The goal is to reach the end of the board first, but the real victory is gaining understanding about privilege and social dynamics through the journey.

Q: What if I land on a difficult card?

A: Difficult cards represent real-world challenges. Use them as opportunities to learn and discuss perspectives with other players.

Q: Can I skip a GUESS space?

A: No, GUESS spaces are mandatory - they're designed to engage players with important topics.

Q: Do I need to remember every card?

A: Not necessarily, but reviewing cards afterward helps reinforce learning.

Q: How many players can play?

A: 2-6 players work best for meaningful interaction and discussion.

Q: What's the purpose of MOVE spaces?

A: They represent unpredictable life events - some help, some hinder. They teach that life outcomes aren't always fair or deserved.
